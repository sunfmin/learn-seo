<p class="lede">You're a developer building marketing automation. So skip the tips-and-tricks framing. The only durable model is this: <b>your content flows through a pipeline of distinct stages, and every SEO/AEO tactic is just an intervention at one stage.</b> Master the stages and every tactic finds its slot.</p>

<Callout variant="win" lead="Your win for this lesson:">given any SEO or AEO tactic, you can name which pipeline and which stage it targets — and therefore what your tooling must produce for it.</Callout>

## Pipeline 1 — Classic search

This is what "SEO" has always meant. Google (or Bing) runs four stages, in order. A page that fails an early stage never reaches the later ones — you cannot rank a page that was never indexed, and you cannot index a page that was never crawled.<sup>[1]</sup>

## Pipeline 2 — AI answer engines

This is "AEO" (a.k.a. GEO). ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Google's AI Overviews don't hand you ten links — they compose one answer and <em>cite</em> a few sources. The stages are different: <b>retrieve</b> candidate passages, <b>synthesize</b> an answer, <b>cite</b> the sources used.<sup>[2]</sup>

<Callout variant="insight" lead="The key connection:">AEO does not replace SEO — it sits on top of it. Most answer engines retrieve from the indexed web (frequently by running a search query under the hood). So being crawlable and indexable is <em>table stakes for both</em>. AEO then adds a new question: once retrieved, is your page easy to extract a clean, quotable, citable fact from?</Callout>

## Why a builder cares: each stage is a thing your software touches

The reason this model is worth memorizing: every stage maps to a concrete artifact your automation will read or write.

| Stage | What your tooling does here |
|-------|------------------------------|
| **Crawl** | Generate/validate `robots.txt` & `sitemap.xml`; check pages return 200 and aren't blocked. |
| **Index** | Render pages headless; verify titles, canonicals, and that content is in the HTML, not hidden behind JS. |
| **Rank** | Audit content depth, internal links, page speed; track keyword positions via an API. |
| **Serve** | Emit `schema.org` structured data so you win rich results & snippets. |
| **Retrieve** | Make facts extractable: clear headings, self-contained sentences, structured data. |
| **Cite** | Track which AI engines cite you, for which prompts, over time. |
